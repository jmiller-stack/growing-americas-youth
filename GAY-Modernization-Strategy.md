## Executive Summary

GAY is doing real, tangible work in Baltimore City — teaching youth to grow food, earn certifications, develop career skills, build futures. The problem: the mission is invisible to the people it exists to serve. The website reads like a grant application, not a youth program. No social media presence, no youth voice, no visual storytelling.

**Core belief:** If young people in Baltimore can't see themselves in your brand within the first 5 seconds, they're gone.

---

## 1. Website Redesign

### Recommended Site Architecture
- **Homepage:** Full-bleed video hero, bold youth-first headline, 3-card value props, testimonials, stats, donor CTA
- **Programs:** Dedicated page per offering (Hydroponics, ServSafe, STEM, Life Skills, Summer Employment)
- **About Us:** Founder story, timeline, board/staff bios, transparent financials
- **Stories / Impact:** Participant stories, photo essays, video profiles, outcome data — update monthly
- **Get Involved:** 4 pathways — youth sign-up, volunteer, donate, partner
- **Contact / FAQ:** Address, map, FAQ answering real youth questions (Is this free? Do I get paid? Can I do this if I have a record?)

### Design Aesthetic for Gen Z
- **Colors:** Rich greens (#2D6A4F, #52B788), warm earth tones, cream backgrounds, vivid accent (yellow-green or coral)
- **Typography:** Bold geometric sans-serif headlines (Space Grotesk, Clash Display, Satoshi); clean body text (Inter, DM Sans)
- **Layout:** Mobile-first, full-width sections, scroll-triggered animations, card-based, feels like a social feed
- **Photography:** Real participants ONLY. No stock photos. Candid shots in the lab.
- **Vibe:** Patagonia meets a Baltimore corner store — grounded, real, local, proud

### Homepage Hero — Rewritten Direction
Lead with what the youth GETS. Direct second-person language. Specific tangible outcomes. Low-commitment CTA. Background: full-bleed video of real participants.

## 2. Brand & Messaging

### Tagline Options
- **"Grow Something Real"** — top recommendation (literal + personal growth + authenticity)

### Mission Statement — Two Versions
- **Youth-facing:** "We teach Baltimore youth to grow their own food, earn real certifications, get paid, and build careers — starting with seeds and water."
- **Funder-facing:** "Growing America's Youth is a Baltimore City 501(c)(3) that equips young people ages 13–24 with hands-on hydroponic agriculture, ServSafe food safety certification, STEM skills, and career development tools..."

### Tone Principles
- Second person always ("You'll learn" not "Our youth will learn")
- Short sentences, active voice, no jargon
- Lead with outcomes, not inputs
- Tell stories not statistics
- Avoid "at-risk" and "troubled" in youth-facing copy

### Youth Voice Framework
- Create Youth Advisory Board (3–5 current/former participants, compensated)
- Collect video testimonials every cohort (phone, natural light, 2–3 min)
- Feature alumni by name on website and social
- Let youth co-create content (supervised social media takeovers)

## 3. Social Media Strategy

### Platform Priority
1. **TikTok** — #1 priority (~80% of Black teens use it daily)
2. **Instagram** — visual credibility, organizational "resume"
3. **YouTube Shorts** — repurpose vertical video, longest content shelf life

### Content Pillars
- **Grow content:** Time-lapse plant growth, hydroponic system walkthroughs
- **Cook content:** "We grew it, now we're cooking it"
- **Science content:** Quick explainers (pH, nutrients, LED lights)
- **Day-in-the-life:** Participant POV from arrival to departure
- **Milestone content:** First harvest, ServSafe cert day, graduation

### 5 Zero-Budget Content Ideas (Film This Week)
1. "You can grow lettuce without soil. Let me show you." — 30-sec walkthrough with trending sound
2. "What I thought vs. what it actually is." — split-screen format
3. "We grew this. Now we're eating it." — harvest to plate, 45 seconds
4. "3 things I learned this week at GAY." — weekly recurring, different participant each time
5. "People don't believe me when I tell them I get paid to garden." — direct-to-camera recruitment video

## 4. Program Modernization

### Features That Make Programs Magnetic
- **Micro-credentials/digital badges** (Credly or Badgr) for completing modules
- **Portfolio project** — documented grow cycle, video, or business plan per participant
- **Cohort identity** — name, color, T-shirt, sense of belonging
- **Guest speakers & field trips** — local chefs, commercial hydroponic farms, UMD Extension
- **Graduation event** — meal from grown produce, certificates, family invited, media

### Connecting to Trending Youth Interests
- **Sustainability/climate:** Hydroponics uses 90% less water — frame as climate action and food justice
- **Cooking culture:** Recipe development workshops, partner with local chefs
- **Entrepreneurship:** Micro-farm business plan module, farmers market sales
- **Health & wellness:** Empowerment framing, not lectures

### Digital/Tech Components
- **Discord server:** Community for participants + alumni (job postings, tips, general chat)
- **YouTube channel:** How-to series, mini-docs, program walkthroughs
- **Grow tracker:** Shared Notion/Google Sheet for logging pH, nutrients, height, yield
- **Digital media skills module:** Teach video editing + content creation; participants produce org content

## 5. Fundraising & Partnerships

### Modern Fundraising Approaches
- **Peer-to-peer:** GiveButter, GoFundMe Charity, Classy — board/alumni create personal pages
- **Recurring micro-donations:** "$10/month keeps one grow station running for a year"
- **Cause marketing/creator partnerships:** Baltimore food bloggers and micro-influencers
- **Employer matching:** Johns Hopkins, T. Rowe Price, Under Armour, CareFirst
- **Storytelling email campaigns:** Mailchimp/ConvertKit free tier, one participant story per month

### Natural Corporate Partners
- **Hydroponic/ag tech:** AeroFarms, Bowery Farming, Gotham Greens, General Hydroponics
- **Food/grocery brands:** Whole Foods, Sweetgreen, Chipotle, Baltimore food co-ops
- **Baltimore institutions:** Johns Hopkins, Morgan State, UMBC, T. Rowe Price Foundation, Annie E. Casey Foundation, Abell Foundation, Baltimore Community Foundation
- **Tech/education:** Google.org, Microsoft Philanthropies, Salesforce.org, Amazon Future Engineer
- **Local restaurants:** Chefs vocal about food equity; potential produce sourcing from GAY

## 6. Implementation Roadmap

### Phase 1 — Foundation (Weeks 1–4)
- Rewrite website copy (homepage + Programs page first)
- Set up Instagram + TikTok with consistent branding
- Film and post the 5 zero-budget content pieces
- Collect photo/video releases from current participants
- Recruit 2–3 youth advisors

### Phase 2 — Build (Weeks 5–12)
- Launch full website redesign (Squarespace or Webflow)
- 3 posts/week on TikTok + Instagram
- Launch YouTube channel with intro video
- Digital badge system live (Credly/Badgr)
- First storytelling email to mailing list
- Outreach to 3–5 corporate partners with one-pager

### Phase 3 — Scale (Months 4–12)
- Discord community server
- Add entrepreneurship + cooking curriculum modules
- First peer-to-peer fundraising campaign
- Creator/influencer partnership
- Apply to Google.org, Microsoft Philanthropies grants
- First cohort graduation event
- Formalize Youth Advisory Board (defined roles + compensation)

---

## Key Insight
GAY has something most nonprofits fabricate: a mission that is visually compelling, immediately tangible, and genuinely life-changing. The mission doesn't need fixing. **The megaphone does.**
